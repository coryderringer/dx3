Learning Task

Overview

For this task, imagine that you work in a hospital, and your job is to make sure that the patients have GOOD outcomes after treatment. You will see two scenarios, each containing information about patients who were treated for a fictional disease. You are responsible for finding out if the different medications are effective or not. Keep in mind that different diseases have different success rates for treatment. Before seeing data about each disease, you will be told which treatment is the most common, and whether the outcomes for the disease are typically GOOD or BAD. For practice, let's look at what would happen if you had a Current Bonus of $0.30, and your next patient had a GOOD outcome.

Overview

For each patient, you will first see whether their outcome was GOOD or BAD. You will be asked to confirm the outcome by clicking the appropriate face. Each GOOD outcome (like this one) adds 186 cents to your bonus. In this example, your Current Bonus would increase from $0.30 to $0.48$0.36. (See "Current Bonus" on the right side.)

Task Description

You will also guess which of two treatments the patient received by clicking on one of the pills below. In this case the options are TW4 or HL3. You will be told the names of the drugs, as well as which one is more commonly used, before each disease scenario.

For now, let's say you guess TW4.

Example Task

After making your guess, you will receive immediate feedback about which drug the patient received. Each time you correctly guess the drug, you will receive a 3 cent accuracy bonus. Your bonus is continually updated in the "Current Bonus" box on the right.

Now that we've seen a GOOD outcome, let's look at a patient who had a BAD outcome.

Overview

This patient had a BAD outcome. Each BAD outcome (like this one) subtracts 186 cents from your bonus. In this example, your Current Bonus would decrease from $0.51$0.39 to $0.33. (See "Current Bonus" on the right side.)

It is theoretically possible for your bonus to be negative. However, it is extremely unlikely that you will end the study with a negative bonus. (If you do end the study with a negative bonus, your bonus will be rounded up to zero.)
